The Article by Lutz – Digital Inequalities in the age of Artificial Intelligence mainly discusses how AI can worsen the current state of the existing digital inequalities. Lutz talked about how AI may be beneficial however if used for terrible means, it can discriminate. Since AI mainly comes from Algorithms that are based on chunks of data, in a part of that data, AI can be discriminative against certain people due to the data it was fed. Week 10

This paper talks about how Artificial intelligence and technology is slowly becoming a new type of colonialization that is colonising Africa. After going through the (Kwet 2019) reading, this new type of colonialisation is called Digital colonialisation, whereby powerful foreign corporations use these advanced technologies to control us and shape our societies to how they want. They want to control us and make us obedient through technological means. They are these huge corporations that aim for the goodwill of the people of Africa while they have different agendas. This paper continues to talk about how AI software can be programmed to be biased and act in interests of those who are in power and discriminate against people in Africa, with darker skin tones, software such as facial recognition can be used as a tool for these foreign countries to control us and favour those who created this software’s through these systems.
